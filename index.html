Abstract

Locomotion and manipulation are two fundamental skills that enable robots to interact with the environment. The integration of these two skills, known as loco-manipulation, has recently attracted significant attention in the robotics community. In this workshop, we will explore the latest developments in loco-manipulation, including planning and control strategies, perception, and machine learning approaches. We will also discuss the challenges that arise when combining these two skills, such as the trade-off between stability and dexterity. Join us for an exciting day of discussions and hands-on activities, and learn how loco-manipulation can help robots better interact with the world around them.
